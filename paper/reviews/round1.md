# Review — Round 1 (2026-05-29)

## Summary

The paper diagnoses the Fisher-information controller of FI-EDL (Fisher-Information EDL) as empirically inert — the gate $\lambda=\exp(-\mathrm{tr}\,I(\alpha))$ saturates near zero because the trigamma-based trace sits at $\mathcal{O}(K)\!\approx\!14.5$ for $K\!=\!10$ — and proposes a deliberately minimal replacement: a Brier-only fit, backbone spectral normalization, and total evidence $\alpha_0$ as the OOD score. On a matched-protocol re-train of six EDL baselines on CIFAR-10/VGG-16, the recipe attains best-mean ECE (3.93%), best-mean SVHN AUROC (92.99) and uniquely-significant CIFAR-100 AUROC (86.32, $p<0.01$ over DAEDL/F-EDL/Re-EDL), with three honest negative results (gate-on collapses calibration to 71.6% ECE; masked-KL is metric-neutral; SN regresses MNIST). The framing is an Occam's-razor simplification of two concurrent SN+evidential methods (DAEDL, Flexible-EDL).

## Strengths

- **Diagnosis is genuinely interesting and well-instrumented.** The finding that $\mathrm{tr}\,I(\alpha)\!\approx\!K$ with per-sample std only 6.5% of the mean, derived from the trigamma form (Eq. 6) and verified empirically (Table tab:gate), is a clean and reusable critique of any FIM-trace-gated regularizer. It is exactly the kind of mechanism-first analysis Q1 reviewers want.
- **Honest statistical framing.** Rare in this literature: the Abstract, §1 contributions, and §1 outline all carry the paired-tie qualification ("best-mean but paired-tied with DAEDL, $p\!=\!0.55$") rather than a "3 of 5 #1" slogan. §5.1 commits to a "win iff $p<0.05$ \emph{and} 95% CI excludes zero" convention, which is well above the journal's typical bar.
- **Three negative results are foregrounded, not buried.** §1 contribution 4 explicitly lists the gate-on collapse, the masked-KL null, and the MNIST SN regression; §6 dedicates a subsection to each. This is the right way to handle a "we removed things and still win" narrative — own what you removed and what it costs you.
- **`tab:rw-concurrent` is the right structural choice.** A side-by-side feature checklist (Backbone SN / Drops KL / Extra heads / Post-hoc density / Distribution) is precisely the format a reviewer who knows DAEDL+F-EDL needs. The conclusion "Ours is the unique entry that adds nothing" is a defensible framing, *if* the empirical wins back it up (largely true, see §6.6.7).
- **Naming-collision footnote.** The careful "we use FI-EDL, F-EDL means Flexible-EDL throughout" disambiguation (lines 115–117, 261–263) preempts a confusion the published abbreviations create.
- **§6 ablation tables are self-contained.** Each table has accuracy + ECE + two OOD AUROCs in the same row, with bolded baseline and explicit seed count in the caption — the reader does not have to flip between tables to read the comparison.

## Weaknesses — major

- **M1. Quantitative inconsistency between Abstract/Intro and Table tab:gate.** The Abstract (line 46) and Highlights (line 74) state $\lambda\!\approx\!5\!\times\!10^{-7}$, and §1 (lines 124–127) repeats this and adds "the effective contribution is *five to seven orders of magnitude* below the fit term". Table tab:gate (lines 444–450) shows **CIFAR-10** $\overline\lambda\!=\!3.8\!\times\!10^{-4}$ and $\lambda_{\max}\!=\!9.3\!\times\!10^{-3}$, three orders of magnitude *larger* than the headline number. Computing $\overline\lambda\!\cdot\!\mathcal{L}_{\text{kl}}\!\approx\!3.8\!\times\!10^{-4}\!\cdot\!66.2\!=\!2.5\!\times\!10^{-2}$ on CIFAR-10, which is only $\sim\!0.7$ of $\mathcal{L}_{\text{fit}}\!=\!0.037$ — i.e. **less than one order of magnitude smaller**, not five to seven. Only the MNIST row reaches the "$5\!\times\!10^{-7}$" headline. This is a flat factual contradiction visible on a single careful read; a sharp reviewer will flag it as evidence of either careless writing or post-hoc inflation. Either (i) restrict the "$\sim\!5\!\times\!10^{-7}$" claim to MNIST and state the CIFAR-10 number honestly ($\overline\lambda\!\sim\!10^{-4}$), or (ii) explain why the CIFAR-10 row is reported as a worst-case max-during-training and the per-step average is closer to $5\!\times\!10^{-7}$ (Table footnote). §4.1 line 463 says "three orders of magnitude below the fit term" which contradicts §1 lines 126–128 — pick one and use it everywhere.

- **M2. Factually wrong "below the EDL baseline" claim.** Both §1 (line 216) and §4.3 (line 579) state: "switching FI-EDL from $\max_c\hat p_c$ to $\alpha_0$ moves the AUROC from 89.89 (**below the EDL baseline**) to 91.72". The report Table 2 §3.3 (line 100) shows the plain-EDL CIFAR-10$\to$SVHN AUROC at maxp is **75.01**, so 89.89 is *above* EDL, not below. The number 89.89 also does not appear in the comparison report (the FI-EDL+SN+alpha0 row is 92.99±1.77, the SN-off+alpha0 row is 91.72±2.21). A reviewer cross-checking will treat this as either a hallucination or a paste from an older table version; both readings damage credibility. Verify 89.89 against the actual FI-EDL-no-SN maxp run, and remove or correct the "below the EDL baseline" phrase. Likewise verify the "$80.6\!\to\!84.67$" CIFAR-100 number on line 581.

- **M3. Highlight 3 overclaims unique significance.** Highlights line 76 reads "New CIFAR-10$\to$CIFAR-100 OOD SOTA AUROC 86.32, significant over DAEDL/F-EDL/Re-EDL." This is correct per `stats_significance.md`. But Highlights line 77 ("Best-mean CIFAR-10 ECE 3.93% and SVHN AUROC 92.99 with paired-tie at the top") is mixed: ECE is paired-tied with DAEDL and with Re-EDL ($p\!=\!0.09$), but **significantly beats F-EDL** ($p\!<\!0.001$). The 85-character Highlight format cannot carry the nuance, but "paired-tie at the top" without specifying *with whom* leaves a reviewer wondering whether you are double-counting wins. Either (a) split into two Highlights, or (b) make Highlight 4 ("Honest limitation: MNIST regression") more prominent so the honesty signal is intact even if the SOTA Highlight is read uncharitably.

- **M4. Title overpromises "Calibrated Uncertainty and OOD Detection" but §1 admits a Misclass loss.** §1 line 164 honestly reports that DAEDL retains a significant 1.29-pp lead on misclassification AUROC. Misclassification detection is the canonical "correct vs incorrect" task within uncertainty estimation; a title that promises "calibrated uncertainty" while losing the misclassification head-to-head is going to be challenged in review. Two acceptable responses: (i) re-title to something narrower (e.g. "for Calibration and Out-of-Distribution Detection on Natural-Image Classifiers"), or (ii) keep the title but explicitly carve out misclassification as a known weakness in the Abstract last sentence. Right now the title implies "all three" while the body owns "two of three".

- **M5. Mechanism explanation for "remove machinery and still beat F-EDL" is missing.** §4.3 (line 552–566) justifies SN by SNGP-DUQ Lipschitz argument, but never explains *why FI-EDL+SN beats F-EDL on CIFAR-10*, given both methods use backbone SN and both are KL-free. The body just asserts "fit/score combination is more effective than head complexity" in §6.6 of the report, but the *manuscript* does not. The closest the body gets is the parenthetical line 274 "(post-hoc density in DAEDL, a different distribution family plus auxiliary heads in F-EDL)". A reviewer with F-EDL/DAEDL background will demand a mechanism: do the extra $(p,\tau)$ heads in F-EDL hurt by adding gradient interference? Does the Flexible-Dirichlet family suffer from a worse $\alpha_0$ score because the total evidence is shared with a mixture component? Without a one-paragraph mechanism story in §4.3 or §7 (deferred), the "we remove things and still win" framing reads as luck rather than insight. This is *the* scoop-defusing risk and it is currently hand-waved.

- **M6. Cross-baseline score audit (contribution 5) is materially unfinished and partly inconsistent.** §1 contribution 5 (lines 213–218) promises "Re-evaluating every evidential baseline with $\alpha_0$ rather than $\max_c\hat p_c$ shifts the leaderboard meaningfully", citing the 89.89→91.72 FI-EDL+SVHN swing. §5.3 is empty (\todo line 648), so the contribution rests on numbers that appear nowhere in the manuscript outside the Introduction. Two problems: (i) per Table 2 §3.3 of the report, DAEDL, Re-EDL, FI-EDL+SN all already use $\alpha_0$ for OOD AUROC, so the "every evidential baseline" claim is partly true only for F-EDL and I-EDL/R-EDL; (ii) the dramatic 89.89→91.72 swing is at FI-EDL specifically, not "every baseline". Rephrase contribution 5 to match what §5.3 will actually show, or commit to evaluating F-EDL/I-EDL/R-EDL at $\alpha_0$ too and reporting the matrix.

- **M7. Reproducibility statement is absent.** No code URL, GitHub placeholder, or data-availability statement is present anywhere — not in §5.1, not in a separate paragraph, not in a footnote. §5.1 lists configs (`configs/paper/comparison_all_cifar10.yaml` etc.) by relative path, which presumes a public repository the reader cannot see. Neurocomputing requires a data/code availability statement and a reviewer-accessible code link (anonymous repo or anonymous GitHub during review). The PAPER_ROADMAP already flags this; the manuscript should add at minimum a "Code and configurations will be released at \url{...} upon acceptance; an anonymized snapshot is available to reviewers" sentence. Compute environment (RTX 3060, total wall-clock $\sim\!50$h per appendix 7.1) should also be summarized — currently zero compute disclosure in §5.1.

- **M8. Single-backbone scope is acknowledged only as a \todo, not as a limitation.** §5.1 line 604–605 contains a `\todo` for ResNet-18 results, which the task brief notes is pending Gap 1. That is reasonable for this round. But the **scoping of the contribution** in §1 and the Abstract does not currently carry any "on VGG-16 / small-ConvNet" qualifier — Abstract line 50 says "a new CIFAR-10 OOD state of the art" full stop, and §1 lines 153–164 generalize over "evidential baselines" without naming the backbone. A reviewer will read this as overclaim until ResNet-18 appears. Until then, add "on VGG-16" to the headline claims in the Abstract and §1, then loosen when ResNet-18 data arrives.

## Weaknesses — minor

- **m1.** Highlights line 74: $\lambda{\approx}5{\times}10^{-7}$ unitless. Should be consistent with the table notation; also see M1.
- **m2.** §4.1 line 458–459: "$\exp(-14.5)\!\approx\!5\!\times\!10^{-7}$" — `exp(-14.5) = 5.04e-7`, fine, but pair it with the table's $\overline\lambda$ values so the reader sees a consistent story.
- **m3.** §5.1 line 631 references "the supplementary statistics file `results/stats\_significance.md`" — Neurocomputing does not accept inline file-path references; this needs to become "Appendix B" or "the supplementary material" once a real supplement is constructed.
- **m4.** §1 line 156 says "three of five metrics first place, one second, one top-tier". "Top tier" is vague; per Table 1 the accuracy of $90.48\!\pm\!0.39$ is statistically tied with I-EDL ($90.58$) and F-EDL ($90.53$) — call this a "tie at the top", not "top-tier".
- **m5.** Algorithm tab:recipe step "(natural-image regime)" appears inside the Training pass step but is not formally a precondition. Move it to a top-line `\KwIn` or `\textbf{Setting:}` so the algorithm is self-contained.
- **m6.** §2.3 (line 306–343) on Calibration and OOD detection is the weakest subsection: it does not cite *any* 2023+ work, despite ODIN/Mahalanobis/SNGP/KNN dating back to 2018–2022. At minimum cite a 2023–2025 calibration survey or a recent train-time calibration method (e.g. label smoothing + mixup variants, or Margin Calibration) to show the writers are aware of the modern landscape. Currently a sharp reviewer can claim the related work is dated.
- **m7.** §3.1 line 358: "non-negative evidence $e(x)\!=\!\mathrm{ReLU}(f_\theta(x))$" — actual codebase may use softplus or exp; verify against `src/models/heads/`. If ReLU is correct, fine; if softplus, fix the body.
- **m8.** §6.3 (`tab:abl-sn`) MNIST baseline accuracy is $97.31\!\pm\!4.44\%$ — a 4.44-pp std on a 97% mean is suspiciously high. - **m9.** `tab:abl-gate-on` and `tab:abl-masked` rows reporting only 2 seeds (no $\pm$) without a $n\!=\!2$ marker in the row itself; the caption mentions "2 seeds for the other rows" but a column-level marker (`†` next to the row label) would make this harder to misread.
- **m10.** Abstract redundantly states "statistically significant gain over DAEDL, F-EDL, and Re-EDL, $p\!<\!0.01$" — `stats_significance.md` shows the lowest p-value for C100 is 0.0072 (vs Re-EDL), so "$p\!<\!0.01$" is correct as the tightest bound but somewhat understates the F-EDL result ($p\!=\!0.0032$). Optional: "$p\!\leq\!0.01$" or "$p\!<\!0.0073$".
- **m11.** §6.1 line 668: "$\alpha_0$ + batch\_z, $\gamma\!=\!0.5$" — the gate is formally defined with $\alpha$ not $\alpha_0$; the manuscript reader has no way to know that "$\alpha_0$ + batch\_z" means "use $\alpha_0$ as the gate signal instead of $\mathrm{tr}\,I(\alpha)$". One sentence of setup before tab:abl-gate-on would fix this.
- **m12.** §1 line 222 "tab:rw-concurrent" body claims "FI-EDL+SN" is the row label, while §4.2 introduces it as "Spectral-normalized, KL-free EDL". Pick one name in body+table+algorithm to avoid the reader doing manual reconciliation.
- **m13.** Refs.bib `evisec2025` entry has author = "Anonymous" — Neurocomputing copy-editing will reject this. Remove until verified or replace with the camera-ready citation.
- **m14.** Refs.bib `chen2024reedl` (Re-EDL) is arXiv-only. If a venue exists by submission time, update; if not, the body needs to note "preprint" once near first citation (line 107).

## Questions for authors

- The §6.1 (tab:abl-gate-on) "fisher+div\_k γ=1, gate-on" row shows accuracy preserved at 89.95% but ECE at 71.62%. The Abstract footnote says this is "severe underconfidence". Have you verified that this is underconfidence specifically (mean predicted prob far below empirical accuracy) and not, e.g., a reliability-diagram failure at the extremes? A two-panel reliability diagram comparing baseline-vs-gate-on would convert this from a one-number claim to a visible mechanism.
- What is the inference cost of FI-EDL+SN vs DAEDL+GMM vs F-EDL (auxiliary heads)? PAPER\_ROADMAP medium-priority TODO mentions this but the manuscript does not. Even a one-row table (FLOPs or wall-clock per CIFAR-10 image) would strengthen the "Occam's razor" framing.
- Is $\alpha_0$ as the OOD score genuinely novel as an *evidential-method default*, or is it the well-known vacuity score with sign-flipped orientation? §4.3 line 573–574 says vacuity $K/\alpha_0$ is monotone-equivalent, which means AUROC/AUPR/FPR95 are identical. If so, contribution 5 should be reframed from "we propose $\alpha_0$" to "we observe that every evidential baseline benefits from switching its default OOD score from $\max_c\hat p_c$ to vacuity/$\alpha_0$, which the literature already knows but the comparison tables in prior papers did not consistently apply".
- The MNIST regression (§6.3): is the inversion of $\alpha_0$ on FMNIST (AUROC 47.04, below chance) reproducible across all 5 seeds, or driven by a subset? If a subset, the "score orientation inverts" mechanism narrative is more nuanced.
- Has the FI-EDL+SN result been validated under a paired comparison on Acc as well? The Misclass loss is real ($-1.29$ pp vs DAEDL), but the accuracy headline ($90.48 \pm 0.39$) is tied with I-EDL/F-EDL — a sentence on this in §5 would strengthen the honesty story.

## Recommendation

**Major revision.**

The diagnosis is original and clean, the experimental design is sound under the matched protocol, and the honest statistical framing is well above the norm for this literature. However, three of the major weaknesses (M1 magnitude inconsistency, M2 "below EDL baseline" factual error, M5 missing mechanism for beating F-EDL) are blocking issues that any careful Q1 reviewer will catch in the first read, and they collectively undermine the credibility of the rest of the paper. The deferred sections (§5.2, §5.3, §7, §8) plus the Gap-1 ResNet-18 results are appropriate next-round work, but the *drafted* content needs to be internally consistent first. With the listed revisions, this paper has a clean path to acceptance.
